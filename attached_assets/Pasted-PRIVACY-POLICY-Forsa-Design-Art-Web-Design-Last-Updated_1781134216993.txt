5. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

We process your Personal Data only where we have a lawful basis under GDPR:

5.1 Consent

**Basis:** You have explicitly consented to Processing.

**Applied to:** Marketing emails, newsletters, non-essential cookies, testimonial use, call recordings, optional data fields.

You may withdraw consent at any time by unsubscribing from communications or contacting us.

5.2 Contract Performance

**Basis:** Processing is necessary to perform a contract with you or to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract.

**Applied to:** Client name, email, phone, billing address, project materials, payment information, service delivery data.

5.3 Legal Obligation

**Basis:** Processing is necessary for compliance with applicable law.

**Applied to:** Tax records, accounting data, fraud prevention, child protection, public safety.

5.4 Legitimate Interest

**Basis:** Processing is necessary for legitimate interests pursued by the Company, except where overridden by your interests, rights, or freedoms.

**Applied to:** Website security, fraud prevention, analytics, customer service improvements, marketing (with legitimate interest assessment), business operations.

Legitimate interests include:
- Preventing fraud and abuse
- Protecting website security and integrity
- Improving website functionality and user experience
- Managing customer relationships
- Conducting business analytics
- Contacting you about updates or service changes

5.5 Vital Interests

**Basis:** Processing is necessary to protect vital interests (e.g., life, safety).

**Applied to:** Emergency situations, child protection, data breaches affecting your safety.

10. COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

10.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that allow websites to recognise and remember you.

10.2 Types of Cookies We Use

**Essential Cookies:**
- Purpose: Maintain website functionality, security, user session management
- Consent: Not required; automatically set
- Duration: Session or up to 12 months
- Examples: Security tokens, CSRF protection, language preference

**Analytics Cookies:**
- Purpose: Understand website usage, visitor behaviour, traffic patterns
- Consent: Required (unless already given)
- Provider: Google Analytics
- Duration: Up to 26 months
- Data: Anonymised usage patterns; no Personal Data collected

**Preference Cookies:**
- Purpose: Remember your choices (theme, language, accessibility settings)
- Consent: Not required
- Duration: Up to 12 months
- Examples: Dark mode preference, language selection

**Marketing Cookies:**
- Purpose: Deliver targeted advertising; track advertising effectiveness
- Consent: Required
- Provider: Google Ads, Facebook Pixel, other ad networks (if used)
- Duration: Up to 12 months
- Notes: Only set if you consent to marketing

10.3 Third-Party Cookies

Third-party service providers (Google, Facebook, etc.) may set their own cookies on your device. These are governed by their privacy policies, not ours.

10.4 Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, we display a cookie consent banner. You may:
- Accept all cookies
- Reject non-essential cookies
- Customise cookie preferences
- Withdraw or change consent at any time

10.5 Disabling Cookies

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but this may affect website functionality and user experience.

10.6 Tracking Technologies

We may also use:
- Web beacons (1x1 pixel images) to track email opens
- Local storage and similar technologies to remember preferences
- Session replay tools (with consent) to improve user experience

11. YOUR RIGHTS UNDER GDPR

As a Data Subject, you have the following rights:

11.1 Right of Access

You have the right to request access to your Personal Data and information about how we process it. We will provide this within 30 days at no cost.

11.2 Right to Rectification

You have the right to correct inaccurate or incomplete Personal Data.

11.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten")

You have the right to request deletion of your Personal Data, except where:
- Processing is necessary for legal obligations
- Processing is necessary for contract performance
- Legitimate interests override your rights
- Data is essential for fraud prevention or security

11.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request that we limit how we process your data while you resolve a dispute or exercise other rights.

11.5 Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive a copy of your Personal Data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.

11.6 Right to Object

You have the right to object to Processing based on legitimate interest, including for marketing purposes. We will cease Processing unless we have compelling legitimate reasons.

11.7 Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority if you believe your rights have been violated.

**UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):** www.ico.org.uk

**Other EU Member State authorities:** Contact your local data protection authority

11.8 Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making or profiling for decisions that significantly affect you.
